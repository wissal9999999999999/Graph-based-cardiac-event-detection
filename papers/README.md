# Papers

This folder contains research papers from Google Scholar, PubMed, IEEE, and ScienceDirect between 2021 and 2024 related to graph signal processing, Research papers on graph ML and GNNs, and ECG analysis.

## List of Papers

### Graph Signal Processing

| Paper Name | Description | Link |
|--------------------------------------|-----------------------------------------------------------------------------------|---------|
|**Graph Signal Processing: History, Development, Impact, and Outlook**|This article provides an overview of the evolution of Graph Signal Processing (GSP), from its origins to its current challenges and applications. It discusses the broad framework of GSP that extends classical signal processing to non-Euclidean domains. The article highlights the impact of GSP on various fields, including data science, neuroscience, image and video processing, and outlines the future directions of GSP. | [PDF Link](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/stamp/stamp.jsp?arnumber=10146241&casa_token=TLHPZr-1WKYAAAAA:6CntjDS4TfLN2k1TChhPqcuon5Zw7JbyVY6sT7PCTBXwGWpppd9J7MKuniDNkJNBi6n_Yh8wAY2hQmc)|
|**Personalized Graph Signal Processing for Collaborative Filtering**|This paper presents a personalized graph signal processing (PGSP) method for collaborative filtering (CF), aiming to improve recommendation accuracy. It introduces personalized graph signals, augmented similarity graphs, and mixed-frequency graph filters to better capture user preferences by incorporating both low-frequency (common interests) and high-frequency (unique interests) components. Extensive experiments demonstrate that PGSP outperforms state-of-the-art CF methods with high training efficiency.|[PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2302.02113)|
| **Graph Signal Processing, Graph Neural Network and Graph Learning on Biological Data: A Systematic Review** | This paper provides a systematic review of graph-based methods such as Graph Signal Processing (GSP), Graph Neural Networks (GNNs), and graph topology inference. It focuses on their algorithms and applications to biological data, offering insights for future research. | [PDF Link](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/9585532) |
| **Modeling Multivariate Biosignals With Graph Neural Networks and Structured State Space Models** |This paper addresses the challenges of modeling spatiotemporal dependencies in multivariate biosignals by representing them as time-dependent graphs. It introduces GraphS4mer, a GNN architecture leveraging Structured State Space architecture and graph structure learning to improve biosignal classification tasks. The model shows improvements in tasks like seizure detection, sleep staging, and ECG classification.|[PDF Link](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v209/tang23a/tang23a.pdf). |
|**A Novel Automated Tower Graph Based ECG Signal Classification Method with Hexadecimal Local Adaptive Binary Pattern and Deep Learning**|This paper proposes a novel ECG signal classification method using a tower graph transformation and a new one-dimensional hexadecimal pattern. The method involves feature extraction with a hexadecimal local adaptive binary pattern and feature selection using iterative ReliefF and Neighborhood Component Analysis (NCA). The approach achieves high classification accuracy with 95.70% and 97.10% using artificial and deep neural networks, respectively.|[PDF Link](https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1007/s12652-021-03324-4.pdf) |
|**A Domain Generative Graph Network for EEG-Based Emotion Recognition** |This paper proposes a novel hybrid model for emotion recognition using EEG signals. It combines generative adversarial networks (GANs) to generate potential representations of EEG signals with graph convolutional neural networks (GCNs) and long short-term memory (LSTM) networks for emotion recognition. The model shows promising results on the DEAP and SEED datasets, outperforming state-of-the-art methods.|[PDF Link](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10035965) |
|**Graph Filters for Signal Processing and Machine Learning on Graphs** |This article provides a comprehensive overview of graph filters, which are essential for extracting information from data residing on graphs. It covers different filtering categories, design strategies, and trade-offs. The article also discusses extending graph filters into filter banks and graph neural networks to enhance their representational power for various signal classes and data patterns. It highlights the importance of graph filters in signal processing and machine learning applications, offering a unifying framework for researchers. |[IEEE Xplore PDF Link](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10388222)|
|**GREAD: Graph Neural Reaction-Diffusion Networks**|This paper introduces GREAD, a Graph Neural Network (GNN) method based on reaction-diffusion equations. GREAD incorporates seven types of reaction equations, including a novel blurring-sharpening equation designed by the authors, to address the oversmoothing problem in GNNs. The model outperforms 28 baselines across 9 datasets, demonstrating superior performance in mitigating oversmoothing and handling various homophily rates.|[PDF (ICML) Link](https://proceedings.mlr.press/v202/choi23a/choi23a.pdf)|
|**EEG Emotion Recognition Using Improved Graph Neural Network with Channel Selection**|This paper proposes a graph neural network (GNN) model with dynamic channel selection for emotion recognition using EEG signals. The model captures intra- and inter-channel features and employs functional connectivity in the graph structure to enhance representation capabilities. Experiments on DEAP and SEED datasets show high accuracy, even with a reduced number of EEG channels, demonstrating the model's effectiveness and efficiency.|[PDF in Computer Methods and Programs in Biomedicine Link](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0169260723000470https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0169260723000470)|
|**Leveraging Visibility Graphs for Enhanced Arrhythmia Classification with Graph Convolutional Networks**|This study explores the use of Visibility Graph (VG) and Vector Visibility Graph (VVG) for representing ECG signals and coupling them with Graph Convolutional Networks (GCNs) for arrhythmia classification. Experiments on the MIT-BIH dataset show that GCNs with VG and VVG can classify arrhythmias effectively without preprocessing. While VG is more efficient, both methods demonstrate promise in improving classification performance. The approach is competitive with baseline methods, but challenges remain, particularly in classifying the S class under the inter-patient paradigm.|[arvix PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.15367)|
### Graph ML and GNNs

| Paper Name | Description | Link |
|------------|-------------|------|
| **NeuroGraph: Benchmarks for Graph Machine Learning in Brain Connectomics** |This paper introduces NeuroGraph, a collection of graph-based neuroimaging datasets tailored for benchmarking Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) in brain connectomics. The datasets enable prediction of behavioral and cognitive traits using static and dynamic brain connectivity data. The study involves extensive exploration and benchmarking of GNN models on these datasets, offering a valuable resource for advancing neuroimaging analysis. | [NeuroGraph PDF link](https://proceedings.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2023/file/14f656f21d09a4114666f60a45aab1aa-Paper-Datasets_and_Benchmarks.pdf) |
|**A Comprehensive Survey on Deep Graph Representation Learning**|This survey comprehensively reviews the state-of-the-art in deep graph representation learning, categorizing existing methods by graph neural network architectures, learning paradigms, and applications. It highlights the advantages of deep learning over traditional methods and covers the essential components of graph representation learning. The survey also discusses future research directions and challenges in the field.|[arXiv PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2304.05055)|
|**A Graph Neural Network-Based Bearing Fault Detection Method**| This paper proposes a method for bearing fault detection using graph neural networks (GNNs). It constructs a graph from the similarity between samples, which is then used by a GNN for feature mapping. The mapped samples are used for fault detection via an ensemble learning approach. Experimental results show a significant improvement in detection accuracy, demonstrating the method's effectiveness.|[Scientific Reports PDF.|[PDF Link](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-023-32369-y.pdf) |
|**Graph Neural Networks in IoT: A Survey**|This survey provides a comprehensive review of recent advances in applying Graph Neural Networks (GNNs) to the Internet of Things (IoT). It discusses the design and application of GNNs in various IoT sensing environments, summarizes public data and source code from collected publications, and explores future research directions. The survey categorizes GNN methods in IoT into multi-agent interaction, human state dynamics, and IoT sensor interconnection.|[PDF Link](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2203.15935) |
|**Fusion of Edge Detection and Graph Neural Networks to Classifying Electrocardiogram Signals**|This paper proposes a method that combines edge detection with graph neural networks (GNNs) for classifying electrocardiogram (ECG) signals. The approach involves converting ECG recordings into waveform images, applying edge detection to create a graph-structured format, and then using GNNs for classification. The method achieves high accuracy and efficiency on the MIT-BIH Arrhythmia and PTB-XL datasets, outperforming various state-of-the-art approaches.|[PDF Link](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0957417423006097)|
|**Graph Neural Networks for Topological Feature Extraction in ECG Classification**|This paper proposes three techniques for classifying ECG signals using deep graph neural networks (GNNs). The methods involve extracting topological features from ECG signals and using the Graph Isomorphism Network (GIN) for classification. Experiments on the PTB Diagnostics dataset show high accuracy, with the three techniques achieving 99.38%, 98.76%, and 91.93% accuracy, respectively.|[arXiv PDF](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2311.04228)|
|**Knowledge Graph Analysis and Visualization of Artificial Intelligence Applied in Electrocardiogram**|This research analyzes the development and application of artificial intelligence (AI) in electrocardiogram (ECG) research using bibliometric and visual knowledge graph methods. The study covers publications from the Web of Science Core Collection until 2021, focusing on co-authorship, co-occurrence, and co-citation of countries, institutions, authors, journals, and keywords. Key findings include the rise in publications and citations in recent years, with China leading in publications and Singapore in average citations per article.|[PDF Link](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/physiology/articles/10.3389/fphys.2023.1118360/full)|
|**Improving Machine Learning Classification of Heart Disease Using the Graph-Based Techniques**|This study aims to enhance the classification of heart diseases using graph-based techniques. Utilizing a dataset of 1190 samples with 23 features, including graph-derived features, various ML models are evaluated. The graph-based model achieves a 95% accuracy, highlighting its ability to capture complex relationships between risk factors and outcomes.|[PDF Link](https://ieeexplore.ieee.org/document/10326233)|
|**Classification of cardiac disorders using weighted visibility graph features from ECG signals**|This study converts ECG signals into weighted visibility graphs and uses the sequence of node weights or diagonals of adjacency matrices as feature sets for ResNet and Inception classifier models. Experiments on the PTB-XL dataset show that the first 3 diagonals of the visibility graph as input to the ResNet model provide superior classification results, achieving a maximum AUC score of 93.46%.|[PDF Link](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S1746809423008534)|
|**A graph-based cardiac arrhythmia classification methodology using one-lead ECG recordings**|This study presents a graph-based methodology for classifying cardiac arrhythmias using single-lead ECG. The visibility graph technique is used to generate graphs from time signals, and informative features are extracted and fed into classifiers. The MLP model achieved the highest performance with an average accuracy of 99.02%.|[PDF Link](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2667305324000607)|




### ECG Analysis

| Paper Name | Description | Link |
|------------|-------------|------|
| **ECG Signal Feature Extraction Trends in Methods and Applications** |This paper reviews common feature extraction techniques used for ECG signal analysis in digital health and AI applications. It covers ECG signal processing and feature extraction in various domains (time, frequency, time-frequency, decomposition, sparse), providing pseudocode for replication. The paper also discusses deep features, machine learning integration, and future work in ECG signal analysis. | [BioMedical Engineering OnLine](https://link.springer.com/content/pdf/10.1186/s12938-023-01075-1.pdf) |
|**Machine Learning-Based Application for Long-Term Electrocardiogram Analysis**|This work involves developing an application to process long-term ECG signals to assist health professionals in decision-making. The application features an interactive interface to view the entire ECG signal as a single image of overlapping cardiac cycles. It also includes email communication for patient follow-up. Tested on artificial and real ECG signals (normal sinus rhythm and arrhythmia from the MIT-BIH database), the results support clinical decision-making.|[PDF Link](https://www.igi-global.com/chapter/machine-learning-based-application-for-long-term-electrocardiogram-analysis/342883)|
|**Adaptive Filter and EMD Based De-Noising Method of ECG Signals: A Review**|This paper proposes a new method combining Empirical Mode Decomposition (EMD), Three iterations of Kalman adaptive filter (TKF), and Customized Otsu Thresholding (COT) for de-noising ECG signals, capable of handling both lower and higher order IMFs, and evaluates its performance on the MIT-BIH arrhythmia database.|[PDF link](https://www.ajmrd.com/wp-content/uploads/2023/03/B530914.pdf)|
|**Honest-GE: 2-step heuristic optimization and node-level embedding empower spatial-temporal graph model for ECG**|This paper proposes a novel graph structure learning method for ECG analysis using heuristic optimization techniques like genetic algorithm and particle swarm optimization. The method enhances model representations with node-level embedding using GraphSAGE, showing significant performance improvements over existing methods.|[PDF link](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0020025524008557)|
|**A compact hybrid fully convolutional and BiLSTM network with squeeze and temporal excitation approach for semantic segmentation of ECG signal**|This paper proposes a novel approach to compress deep architecture for ECG wave segmentation, addressing issues of computational burden and overfitting. The method combines convolutional and BiLSTM networks with a filter squeeze and temporal excitation approach.|[PDF link](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s11042-022-13821-z)|
